Congratulations to Suhas Yathiraj, Nitesh Kumar, Nithya Sre Sivan, Manisha Ramadass, and Thulasimathi Murugesan for their remarkable achievements at the 2024 Paris Paralympics! Suhas Yathiraj earned a silver medal, Nitesh Kumar won gold, Nithya Sre Sivan and Manisha Ramadass both secured bronze, and Thulasimathi Murugesan claimed silver. Their exceptional performances in badminton have brought great pride to India, showcasing their skill, dedication, and significant contributions to the sport.

Thulasimathi Murugesan (Silver, Badminton): Indian shuttlers achieved remarkable success on September 2 at the Paris Paralympics. Thulasimathi Murugesan secured a silver medal in the women's singles (SU5) badminton event, after falling to China's Qiu Xia Yang in the final at Paris' Porte de La Chapelle Arena.

Manisha Ramadass (Bronze, Badminton): Manisha Ramadass is an Indian para-badminton player in the SL3 classification. At the 2024 Paris Paralympics, she won a bronze medal, showcasing her exceptional skill and contributing to India’s success in para-badminton. Her achievements in national and international tournaments have solidified her reputation as a top competitor in her sport.

Nitesh Kumar (Gold, Badminton): Nitesh Kumar clinched a gold medal for India in badminton at the 2024 Paris Paralympics. Competing in the men’s singles event, Kumar’s exceptional performance highlighted his skill and determination, securing a top spot on the podium and adding to India’s prestigious achievements in para-badminton.

Suhas Yathiraj (Silver, Badminton): Suhas Yathiraj won a silver medal for India in badminton at the 2024 Paris Paralympics. Competing in the men's singles SL4 category, Yathiraj's remarkable performance earned him a place on the podium, showcasing his exceptional skill and contributing significantly to India's success in para-badminton.

Nithya Sre Sivan (Bronze, Badminton): Nithya Sre Sivan won a bronze medal for India in badminton at the 2024 Paris Paralympics. Competing in the women’s singles event, Sivan’s outstanding performance earned her a podium finish, adding to India’s achievements in para-badminton and highlighting her skill and determination.
