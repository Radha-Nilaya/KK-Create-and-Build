Borivali to Virar Railway Track Expansion: Western Railway Initiates Land Acquisition for New Lines

In a major step to alleviate pressure on the current railway network, Western Railway, in partnership with the Palghar collectorate, has commenced the land acquisition process for adding the fifth and sixth railway lines between Borivali and Virar, as reported by Hindustan Times (HT). These new lines are expected to substantially ease congestion on the fast corridor, which will be exclusively designated for suburban train services.

The Palghar district administration, under the leadership of Collector Govind Bodke, has initiated the acquisition of 122 properties in the Vasai tehsil to support the project. Bodke stated in Hindustan Times (HT), "We have already begun the process of acquiring the necessary properties." He also noted that plans are underway to expand the platforms at Vasai station to handle the anticipated increase in traffic.

He further indicated that the acquisition will encompass residential areas, commercial properties, and saltpan lands, including some properties already owned by the railways.

On August 7, Western Railway released a public notice about the acquisition of properties in several areas, including Divanman, Achole, Navghar, Manikpur, and Umela. The identified land for acquisition includes a combination of housing societies and agricultural plots.

The project, an integral part of the Western Railway corridor, has been approved with an estimated budget of Rs 2,184.02 crore. Key components, including estimates, designs, and drawings, have been given the green light. A detailed drone survey has been completed, and the alignment for the new lines has been finalized.

Additionally, the plans for all major and minor bridges required for the project have been approved. The Mumbai Rail Vikas Corporation (MRVC) is tasked with executing the project, with Western Railway aiming for a completion date of December 2027. Work on related infrastructure is already in progress, and contracts have been awarded for constructing essential service buildings and railway quarters. Current projects include building the relay room at Mira Road, the signal office, the tool room, the RRI building, and the Railway Protection Force Barrack at Dahisar, as well as the station building at Naigaon and railway quarters at Vasai Road and Bhayander.

According to an MRVC spokesperson, the tender for relocating structures was awarded on October 16 of the previous year, and work is currently underway. Construction and modifications of Foot Over Bridges and platforms have also started, following the awarding of contracts in early February.

Additionally, tenders for constructing major and minor bridges, road overbridges (ROBs), and the associated earthworks for the proposed embankment were invited on June 6, marking a significant advancement in the project's development.
